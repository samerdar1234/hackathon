Why Talk About Mental Health?

Poor mental health is a huge issue impacting the metro Detroit area, however, within this population, children and teenagers are the most affected. In a community that does not prioritize good mental health, young people feel silenced. Whether because of trauma, fear, or the lack of support from the people around them, young people are struggling to voice how poor mental health is negatively impacting them.

Mental health problems affect about 1 in 10 children and young people. The biggest problems affecting these young people include depression anxiety, and conduct disorder, and these problems are often a direct response to what is happening in their lives.(Mental Health Foundation). Staying quiet about these problems only enhances the damages these problems cause on a young person’s life. Unfortunately, even if a young person tries to call for help, often they do not receive the necessary care they need to counteract these mental health issues.


Common Causes of Poor Mental Health
  • Abusers
  • Sex Offenders
  • Drugs
  • Bullies
  • Death of a Loved One
  • A Stressful Living Situation

It is Okay to say, “I’m Not Okay”

The list above is not a complete list. Anyone can suffer from poor mental health for any number of reasons. Student-aged children are especially vulnerable to mental health problems because of their lack of control in their lives at that age and because the stigmatism against mental health prevents students from feeling comfortable talking about an issue they may have. So pay attention. If your friend or child has any signs that their mental health may be suffering, reach out to them. Many times, young people are afraid of asking for help or even accepting that they have a problem. By reaching out, you can make a positive impact on your loved one’s life.


Signs You Should Reach Out:
  • Confused thinking or reduced ability to concentrate
  • Excessive fears or worries
  • Extreme feelings of guilt
  • Extreme mood changes of highs and lows
  • Withdrawal from friends and activities
  • Significant tiredness
  • Low energy or problems sleeping
  • High anxiety

How Can this Upcoming School Year Affect Mental Health

This upcoming school year can affect mental health even more than the previous years. The fear of getting horribly sick now accompanies the other stresses school provides such as academic pressures, social pressures, and the mental pressures. For students, do not be afraid to reach out. School may be accompanied by pressure, but it also provides a network to help deal with those pressures. Your counselors, teachers, trusted adults, and friends will all be there to help you through your fears and anxieties. You are not alone. Everyone has worries, but by reaching out and communicating with your support network, your negative emotions can be alleviated. Poor mental health does not define who you are or who you can be.
